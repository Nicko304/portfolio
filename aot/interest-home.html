Non-stop Action

Anime comes in numerous different forms and shapes, often involving fictional worlds with unique concepts. Attack on Titan (AOT) is one of these shows, that starts off with the last of humanity surviving against man-eating monsters called titans. However, it quickly evolves as the world continues building, characters are explored, and mysteries behind the titans are slowly revealed. Throughout the entire journey, there is constant development and many challenges are thrown at the main cast one after another.

AOT is fairly fast-paced as the show rarely wastes time. Every scene has its purpose, even when it is not clearly shown. In the later seasons, there are many moments that show hidden parts or new takes of previous scenes, bringing a whole new meaning to them and the implications of some character's actions. Throughout the entire series, the main cast deals with many challenging walls and when they are dealing with one huge conflict, they are still working towards their overarching goal of finding more about titans to stop them for good.

Thematic Exploration

Under the surface of many shows, the viewer is often challenged to think about the implications and morality of actions throughout shows. An example would be in Attack on Titan, it appears to be about fighting back against man-eating titans. Without diving too much into spoilers, it eventually grows into complete war, subjecting characters to commit actions that will make you question who you should support. No single side is shown to be completely perfect by the end of the series as the evil try to turn good and the good turn to evil. There are many ways that conflict can be resolved in the show, but every solution has its own benefits, drawbacks, risks, morality, and conflicting support involved. The show goes so much deeper while many other shows have their own themes to dive in while providing great entertainment and beautiful scenes.

The show can also be viewed as a tragedy since much of the conflict could be completely prevented if only a few events in the past happened differently. One example is that much of the xenophobia later in the show was caused by a single man abusing power with a twisted sense for dominating. His sense for domination led to growing hatred for his people, even though many of them were unwilling (but forced) to follow their current king. That hatred kept growing for centuries, eventually manifesting into all descendants of that race being outcasted and severely mistreated by the rest of the world. Even with him long dead, much of the world refused to come to reason with the race, and only grew their hatred as the race began defending themselves against the rest of the world, resulting in monumental conflict. The race did not wish to fight but whenever they attempted to reason with the rest of the world, they would be attacked and shunned until some members felt they had no other options left, but violence.

Top-tier Music

As with mostly all animes, AOT has a number of opening and ending sequences for each part of a season. Every opening and ending has stunning music paired with top-tier animation for viewers to enjoy every single episode with the exception of one ending sequence. In addition to being great to listen to, these sequences often bring a sneak peek into what the current portion of the season will be about, but there is also plenty of original content presenting cool hypotheticals, such as the numrerous type of animal titans that could have existed years before the series takes place. As for that one ending sequence being an exception, it is often seen as nightmare fuel but is packed with details and backstory of the world of titans that are not revealed until much later in the series. When combined with the ominous music, the ending is still very captivating and provides a lot of content to speculate about, even years after its release.

Asied from openings, AOT is filled with many outstanding original sound tracks (OSTs) that are played during key scenes and sequences. Most, if not all, of these OSTs are great to listen to but they also greatly enchance the impact and intensity of scenes throughout the show. These OSTs really help make the viewer become more immersed in the scenes for greater enjoyment.
